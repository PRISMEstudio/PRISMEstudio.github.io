USAGE WARNING

Warning.

For some people, the use of this game requires special precautions:

At the request of the decree of November 2, 1994 ordering the posting of warnings on the packaging and instructions for use of video games as well as in establishments making video games available to the public by French law .

Read this before using a video game by yourself or your child. Some people are susceptible to epileptic seizures or to fainting when they see certain types of flashing lights or things that are common in our daily environment. These people are at risk of seizures when they watch certain television images or when they play certain video games. These phenomena can appear even though the subject has no medical history or has never been confronted with an epileptic seizure.

If you or a member of your family have already experienced symptoms related to epilepsy (seizure or loss of consciousness) in the presence of light stimulation, please consult your doctor before use. We advise parents to be mindful of their children when playing video games. If you or your child experiences any of the following symptoms: dizziness, blurred vision, twitching of the eyes or muscles, loss of consciousness, orientation disturbances, involuntary movement or seizures, please stop playing immediately and seek medical advice. doctor.

Precautions to be taken in all cases when using a video game:
- When using a video game connectable to a screen, play a safe distance away from that television screen and as far as the connection cord allows.
- Preferably use video games on a small screen.
- Avoid playing if you are tired or lack sleep.
- Make sure you play in a well-lit room.

In use, take ten to fifteen minute breaks every hour.
